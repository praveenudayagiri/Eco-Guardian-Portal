Act 1: Environmental Protection Act

Year: 1970

Summary: The Environmental Protection Act was enacted in response to increasing concerns about environmental pollution and degradation. It provides the framework for regulating pollutants and protecting the environment and public health.

Act 2: Clean Air Act

Year: 1970

Summary: The Clean Air Act aims to control air pollution by regulating emissions of hazardous air pollutants, establishing air quality standards, and implementing programs to reduce pollution from mobile and stationary sources.

Act 3: Clean Water Act

Year: 1972

Summary: The Clean Water Act is a federal law that regulates the discharge of pollutants into navigable waters and aims to restore and maintain the chemical, physical, and biological integrity of the nation's waters.

Act 4: Endangered Species Act

Year: 1973

Summary: The Endangered Species Act aims to protect and recover endangered and threatened species and their habitats. It provides for the conservation of ecosystems upon which threatened and endangered species depend.

Act 5: Resource Conservation and Recovery Act

Year: 1976

Summary: The Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) regulates the management of hazardous waste from generation to disposal. It aims to protect human health and the environment from the potential hazards of waste disposal.

Act 6: Safe Drinking Water Act

Year: 1974

Summary: The Safe Drinking Water Act (SDWA) is a federal law that protects public drinking water supplies throughout the nation. It aims to ensure safe drinking water for the public by regulating the quality of drinking water and its sources.

Act 7: Toxic Substances Control Act

Year: 1976

Summary: The Toxic Substances Control Act (TSCA) regulates the manufacture, distribution, use, and disposal of chemicals in the United States. It aims to protect human health and the environment from potential risks associated with chemical substances.

Act 8: National Environmental Policy Act

Year: 1969

Summary: The National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) establishes a national policy for protecting the environment. It requires federal agencies to consider the environmental effects of their proposed actions and involves the public in the decision-making process.

Act 9: Oil Pollution Act

Year: 1990

Summary: The Oil Pollution Act (OPA) aims to prevent and respond to oil spills that occur in U.S. waters. It establishes liability and compensation mechanisms for oil spill cleanup and damages, as well as requirements for oil spill prevention and response planning.

Act 10: Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (CERCLA)

Year: 1980

Summary: The Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (CERCLA), also known as Superfund, provides for the cleanup of hazardous waste sites and the allocation of liability for cleanup costs. It aims to protect human health and the environment from hazardous substances.

Act 11: Marine Mammal Protection Act

Year: 1972

Summary: The Marine Mammal Protection Act (MMPA) aims to protect marine mammals and their habitats. It regulates the taking, import, and export of marine mammals and requires conservation measures to ensure their survival and recovery.

Act 12: Clean Power Plan

Year: 2015

Summary: The Clean Power Plan was a set of regulations aimed at reducing carbon dioxide emissions from existing power plants in the United States. It sought to promote cleaner energy sources and combat climate change by reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Act 13: Renewable Fuel Standard

Year: 2005

Summary: The Renewable Fuel Standard (RFS) mandates the blending of renewable fuels, such as ethanol and biodiesel, into transportation fuel sold in the United States. It aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, promote energy independence, and support the development of renewable fuels.

Act 14: Kyoto Protocol

Year: 1997

Summary: The Kyoto Protocol is an international treaty aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions and combating climate change. It sets binding emission reduction targets for developed countries and establishes mechanisms for emissions trading and clean development.

Act 15: Paris Agreement

Year: 2015

Summary: The Paris Agreement is an international treaty that aims to limit global warming to well below 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit the temperature increase to 1.5 degrees Celsius. It outlines commitments by countries to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and enhance climate resilience.

Act 16: Montreal Protocol

Year: 1987

Summary: The Montreal Protocol is an international treaty aimed at protecting the ozone layer by phasing out the production and use of ozone-depleting substances, such as chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) and hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs). It has been successful in reducing ozone depletion and mitigating the harmful effects of ultraviolet radiation.

Act 17: National Wildlife Refuge System Administration Act

Year: 1966

Summary: The National Wildlife Refuge System Administration Act establishes a national network of wildlife refuges for the conservation and management of fish, wildlife, plants, and their habitats. It aims to protect and enhance wildlife populations and provide opportunities for wildlife-dependent recreation and environmental education.
